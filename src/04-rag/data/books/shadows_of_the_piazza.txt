The fog in Venice didn't just obscure the canals; it swallowed secrets. Inspector Moretti stood by the Rialto Bridge, clutching a damp envelope that felt heavier than the lead weights used by the local fishermen.

Inside was a single charred coin from the fall of Constantinople. How it ended up in the pocket of a modern-day gondolier was a riddle that led straight to the Doge’s hidden chambers.

The water lapped against the stone, a rhythmic reminder that some things are better left submerged.
