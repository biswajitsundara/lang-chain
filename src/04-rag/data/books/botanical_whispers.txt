Mycorrhizal networks, often referred to as the "Wood Wide Web," represent one of nature's most sophisticated communication systems. Through these fungal threads, trees exchange nutrients and chemical signals.

In our study of the Amazonian basin, we discovered that older "Mother Trees" actually prioritize nutrient delivery to their own kin, suggesting a level of biological intelligence.

These networks allow the forest to act as a single, cohesive organism rather than a collection of individual plants competing for sunlight.
